## Colloquial particles — the real syllabus

These get zero coverage in most textbooks but appear constantly in this transcript. This is the highest-leverage vocabulary in the whole file because you'll hear it in *every* future conversation with this family.

| Particle | Function | Example from the transcript |
|---|---|---|
| **kan** | seeking agreement / "right?" | "Tahu kan berapa nih harganya?" |
| **sih** | softener, mild emphasis/curiosity | "Apa sih itu konsepnya?" |
| **dong** | insistence, "come on" | "Salam dulu, Sin, ajak dia ngomong Indonesia... coba Sin." (also "dong" alone as in "Password lagi dong") |
| **loh / lho** | mild surprise, "you know" | "Ih, makanya kupingmu itu loh, jangan pakai gituan." |
| **nih** | "here", drawing attention | "Ini terus belok kanan nih." |
| **kok** | "how come / why" (surprise/complaint) | "Kok tadi udah deket?" / "Kok aneh." |
| **deh** | softening/conceding | (listen for it in future recordings — not heavily used in Conv. 1) |
| **ya / iya** | "yeah/right", constant backchannel | everywhere |
| **nggak / enggak** | "not/no" (colloquial for *tidak*) | everywhere |
| **udah / belum** | "already / not yet" (colloquial for *sudah/belum*) | "Udah, udah." / "Belum update Netflix-nya." |
| **gitu / begitu** | "like that" | "Oh, gitu." / "Semangat, gitu loh." |
| **banget** | "very/so" (intensifier) | "Asik banget." / "Manis banget." |
| **mendingan** | "better/rather" | "Blue Sky, mendingan." |
| **entar / nanti** | "later" | "Entar dulu, tak tanya." |
| **makanya** | "that's why" | "Makanya Mama aja nggak berani ke sini dulu." |
| **atuh / mah** | **Sundanese** particles, softeners/topic markers — not standard Indonesian | "Itu mah mau buat airdrop atuh." |

Rule of thumb: if you can't map a short word to a dictionary entry, it's probably a particle carrying tone, not meaning. Learn its *function*, not a translation.

## Cultural/context notes

- **Suku (ethnic group) talk (~1:00:00):** family has mixed heritage across Bali, Lombok (Sasak — mis-transcribed as "sah-sah"), Madura, and Java. Worth understanding suku ≠ province; it's ethnic/cultural lineage.
- **Royal ancestry story:** great-grandfather was pushed out of Balinese royalty for converting to Islam and taking multiple wives — historically common at the time.
- **Marriage law tangent:** modern Indonesia caps polygamy at 4 wives (historically), and infidelity — including "sweetheart cheating" (texting-only) — can now be legally actionable and can cost someone their job/rank, especially in government positions.
- **Salim**: the hand-to-forehead greeting gesture for elders — Devin prompts a kid to do it ("Salim dulu, Salim.").
- **Assalamualaikum**: used casually as a greeting when arriving/leaving, consistent with a Muslim household.
- **Soniox itself is a running joke/topic** — the family is amused/impressed by it, references it repeatedly ("distranscript" as a tongue-twister, "Aho. Aho. It transcribed that too.").

## Comprehension checkpoints (use these to self-test)

Cover the English gloss and see if you can produce/understand the Indonesian, or vice versa:

1. "Kamu mau download di anu, di laptopmu?" — *Do you want to download it on your laptop?*
2. "Kalau yang di Pertamini itu dia tutup kalau sore." — *The one at the Pertamini stall closes in the afternoon/evening.*
3. "Ini terus belok kanan, yang di belakang ini." — *Keep going, turn right, it's the one behind here.*
4. "Sudah semakin lancar." — *[Your Indonesian] is getting more fluent already.* (said about Devin — good encouragement to remember)
5. "Kalau Dila nggak pakai bahasa Inggris dong, Dila tidak praktis Inggris." — *If Dila doesn't use English, she won't get to practice English.*
6. "Tahu kan berapa nih harganya?" — *You know how much this costs, right?*
